LatentVLA: Enhancing Long-Horizon Vision-Language-Action Models with a Latent World Model

Anonymous Authors

Abstract

Learning an accurate world model to predict future states is fundamental for Vision-Language-Action (VLA) agents to achieve long-horizon reasoning and effective planning. However, prevailing world models predominantly operate in raw pixel space, which presents two critical challenges: (1) high-dimensional observations introduce significant task-irrelevant perceptual noise, leading to perceptual overfitting, and (2) the prohibitive computational cost of pixel-level autoregressive prediction limits the feasible modeling horizon.

In this paper, we propose LatentVLA, a novel paradigm that performs world modeling in a highly compressed latent space. By leveraging a lightweight Adaptive Attention-based Pooling mechanism, we distill high-dimensional visual features into a single, semantically-rich latent world token per frame. This approach significantly reduces computational complexity, enabling the model to scale stably to long-horizon planning steps. Unlike pixel-level models, LatentVLA operates in a high-level semantic space to effectively decouple environmental dynamics from perceptual noise. Across LIBERO, Metaworld, and real-world Piper tasks, LatentVLA with lightweight LoRA fine-tuning on a π0 base surpasses the significantly π0.5 model. This performance gap highlights the efficacy of our semantic abstraction mechanism over traditional scaling.
